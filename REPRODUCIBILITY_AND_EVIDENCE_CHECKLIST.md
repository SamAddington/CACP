# Reproducibility and Evidence Checklist

Before submitting the paper or artifact, confirm the following:

- [ ] The paper states whether the dataset is original, fresh rerun, or synthetic/reconstructed.
- [ ] The analyst timing method is described clearly.
- [ ] Analyst timing data are real if the paper claims a human analyst study.
- [ ] The exact prompts are included or the paper says prompts are reconstructed.
- [ ] The model/version manifest identifies model, provider, date, temperature, tool-calling mode, and whether latency is measured or modeled.
- [ ] Raw logs are included or clearly labeled as reconstructed examples.
- [ ] The claim of 42% MTTA reduction is reconciled with the displayed values 8.6s and 4.9s.
- [ ] Hashes/checksums are regenerated after final edits.
- [ ] Any privacy-sensitive log content is removed or replaced with simulated values.
